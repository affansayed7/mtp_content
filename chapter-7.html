Why UPF exists
Without UPF, nobody answers these questions:
Who assigns IPs? Who does NAT? Who enforces QoS? Who isolates slices? Who collects traffic stats?
UPF = Router + Gateway + NAT device + Traffic Manager — all in one
UPF responsibilities
01
Packet forwarding
All user traffic passes through — ping, YouTube, iperf3, camera streams. Every single packet.
02
NAT
Translates private UE IP (10.45.0.x) to host public IP so packets can reach internet and return.
03
QoS enforcement
Prioritizes traffic — emergency/URLLC traffic over best-effort eMBB traffic.
04
Slice enforcement
Enforces traffic isolation between slices. Slice A traffic must not bleed into Slice B.
05
Traffic steering
Decides where packets should be forwarded — internet, MEC server, or another UPF.
06
Statistics collection
Reports per-flow stats to SMF — used for billing, monitoring, your iperf3 measurements.

Viva questions
Q: What is the role of UPF in 5G?
UPF is the sole user-plane component — it forwards all user packets, performs NAT, enforces QoS and slice isolation, and collects traffic statistics.
Q: Why might a shared UPF become a bottleneck?
Multiple slices compete for the same CPU, memory, buffers, and bandwidth inside one UPF. Heavy traffic from one slice can increase latency, jitter, and packet loss for other slices.
Q: What are the trade-offs of dedicated UPF?
Better isolation and predictable performance — but requires more hardware, higher cost, and greater management complexity.
Q: Registration succeeds but UPF is down. What happens?
PDU session fails — UE gets no IP. Even if SMF runs, without UPF there is no data path and no internet access.
Q: Which component controls UPF in 5G?
SMF controls UPF via PFCP (Packet Forwarding Control Protocol) — SMF is control plane, UPF is user plane.
⭐ UPF is the user-plane gateway responsible for packet forwarding, NAT, QoS, and slice enforcement. Shared UPF is cost-efficient but risks resource contention under load. Dedicated UPF provides isolation but at higher cost. Your thesis measures exactly where this trade-off breaks down.
